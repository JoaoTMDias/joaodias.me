## The Problem

Picture this: It is January of 2021 and, In Portugal, people are starting to face the reality of having opened stores and relaxed a bit on Christmas and New year's eve. Hospitals are becoming full of patients in serious respiratory conditions, ambulances queuing at the entrances in every health facility, older and younger dropping like flies. The images we've seen before in Italy and Spain, they were now happening here. No place to bury the dead, no one know what was going to happen next.

A vaccine was yet to be given to the population, so everyone was locked up at home, scared about what turned out to be a violent outbreak of Covid-19 that took almost two months to pass.

We all knew that this virus was something not be taken lightly. TVs were constantly blasting news about the numbers of infected and the dead. All the time, everytime, everywhere.

At the same time, on social media, there was also this frustrating vibe of toxic-positivity, with 'everything's going to be allright' and 'we're all in this together'.

There were a lot of us that knew that this trend was bullshit. We prefer the truth, not some made up falsehoods about the status of the pandemic or, on the other hand, over-exaggerated news coverage.

Health care professionals were in dire situations, exhausted, scared and not being well taken care of by the government. People in other areas such as arts and culture were out of work since 2020 - no live concerts, no theatre, not cinema, no nothing. Many of them and their families were in very hard situations.

These people all needed to vent their opinions.

She reached out to Henrique Macedo (a friend of mine) and me to develop a website for the conference

## The Solution

She asked us for a website where the topic was serious, but the visuals were not. So, something fun. And fun we had, indeed.

For the tools, we went with Next.js, Styled Components and Cypress. I think we spent like a weekend working on this. Henrique is a friend of mine, we've known each other since our college days, and we work very well together. He developed the visuals for the whole branding/website and I did most of the implementation and testing.

Given the opportunity, I came up with the idea to add a couple of easter-eggs on the website, something that only developers and curious people would get to, but would help to spread-out the word about the webiste.
